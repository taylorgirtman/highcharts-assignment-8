Public principals v. Private principals

By Taylor Girtman

Public and private schools have clear differences in demographics and outcomes. Private school students have greater family incomes. According to data from a 2011-2012 Schools and Staffing Survey by the U.S. Department of Education, a majority of public and private school principals' highest degree obtained is a masters degree. However, there are more private school principals with only bachelors degress than in public schools. The graph below shows these differences.

Source: U.S. Department of Education

When salaries of private and public school principals are compared, public school principals have a greater salaries on agerage. According to data from three school years, private-school principals in their first three years make about $10,000 less than an average principal. Public school principals have a lesser average margin of difference.

Source: U.S. Department of Education
